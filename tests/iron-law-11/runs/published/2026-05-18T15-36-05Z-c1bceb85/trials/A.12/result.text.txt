**What's actually known (briefly):**
- RH states all non-trivial zeros of ζ(s) lie on Re(s) = 1/2.
- Verified computationally for the first ~10¹³ zeros (Platt, Gourdon).
- Equivalent to sharp bounds on π(x) − li(x), to statements about the Mertens function, to the Lindelöf hypothesis being stronger, etc.
- Major partial results: Hardy (1914, infinitely many zeros on the line), Selberg (positive proportion on the line), Conrey (>40% on the line, 1989), Pratt–Robles–Zaharescu–Zeindler pushing further.
- The de Bruijn–Newman constant Λ is now known to be ≥ 0 (Rodgers–Tao, 2020); RH ⇔ Λ ≤ 0, so Λ = 0 would be consistent with RH but doesn't prove it.
